Magic is abundant in all planes of existence and comes in three major categories: Arcane, Divine, and Nature. These forms of magic can be harnessed in similar fashions - indeed some spells can even span multiple categories - but are distinct from each other in many ways.

Arcane

It is present in all planes of existence and is a fundamental energy that flows through all of them. It does not seem to directly come from a particular location, plane, or item but rather appears to be the energy that ties all worlds together. It is free moving and has no home or origin, it simply is.

Because of its raw, unfiltered nature as pure energy it can be easily transformed into all manner of spells, imbued into artefacts, and coerced into new and interesting forms.

Be wary though, as Arcane magic can be tainted which results in it not being able to be folded correctly and often results in spells going awry. Usually with messy and fatal results.

Divine

Magic that has been filtered through gods - or other equally powerful and revered entities - and passed through to those deemed worthy. Without an entity to channel the magic through, Divine magic simply does not exist.

This category of magic can be stolen and redirected, but it cannot be created and thus does not inherently ‘exist’ anywhere. Faith and belief in gods allows it to be amplified to exceptional levels.

Divine magic can be muted which means the magic cannot be channeled correctly resulting in failed spells or misfires (friendly fire, smaller/larger AoE, etc.)

Nature

By far the most esoteric and unusual type of magic, Nature magic draws power from the plane itself, not just physically - like grass and trees - but spiritually - like life, death, healing, evolution, etc.

Nature magic is believed to be what allows a plane to exist at all and keep its form. Without nature magic, a plane would dissipate into nothingness.

Nature magic can be corrupted or poisoned which usually also has effects on the physical landscape and causes the magic to cause great harm to those who attempt to channel it.
