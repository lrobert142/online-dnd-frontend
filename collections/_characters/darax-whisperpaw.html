One of the younger members of the pack at only 253 years of age, he prefers to spend most of his time in his quadrupedal wolf form swimming and playing with the various bones left behind after the pack's meals. Ever impulsive and aloof, he tends to speak before thinking which has seldom gotten him in trouble with Vargus. Still, his actions are never intended to cause irritation, and he makes up for it with his intense loyalty and unnatural stealth, being one of their most talented and effective scouts despite outward appearances.

Though always eager for a good roll in the hay - even with those outside the pack, provided they are trusted by Vargus - he is no fool and knows better than to gift the Blessing to anyone else. As such, despite his casual attitude he is incredibly responsible. When he needs to be.

During the party's venture to the lycanthrope's den, his eye seemed particularly drawn to Sýnchysi, though the exact reasons were unknown. Perhaps he admires someone as tall as he is or the scent of the Feywilds?

Hearing a howl of need from Petee, Darax made the split-second decision to sprint into town and help the party slay the cultists assaulting Wolfhaven. Though successful in slaying them and mitgating casualties, it also broke the stealth that his pack had secretly held for many decades. He is afraid of what might come of this; both what the villagers might think, and what Vargus will say.

After the party was able to convince the residents of Wolfhaven to accept the lycanthropes instead of fearing them, Darax's mood improved significantly and he has been much happier spending time in town interacting with all he can. He particularly seems excited to "begin a new life with his pack, one that doesn't need us to hide in the shadows forever."
