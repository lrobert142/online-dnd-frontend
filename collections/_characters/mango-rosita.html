An old friend of Petee's who now runs the best - and only - inn in Wolfhaven, she is always one for rumours and stories, no matter how absurd or outlandish they are. Though she does have a much better fondness for true stories from adventurers and hopes that one day Wolfhaven will grow big enough that adventurers will frequent her inn and regale her with wonderful tales. So far it has only been a few travelling merchants.

She is easy-going, very kind, always sees the good in life no matter how dire a situation seems and refuses to let anyone bring the mood down. Her upbeat, persistent optimism and happy-go-luck view on life - and her soothing excited chirps - can bring a smile to even the most gruff farmer.

She is the beloved wife of Ginger Rosita.

Upon meeting the party for the first time and giving them food and a room for the night, she encountered Fari - Sýnchysi's Fey Blinkhound - and was immediately smitten with how adorable he is and how helpful around the inn he is. She spent a good period of time cooing over him and giving him a lot of quite lavish treats, probably more than he should have had in one sitting.

"But he's such a good boy, I just can't rightly say no to his big ol' puppy-dog eyes. Look at them! Just one more? Pleeeeease!"

A quick cultist blade to the heart was all it took to drain the life from her body. But even in the beyond, she could feel Ginger's broken heart and her undying love for her refused ot let go of this mortal coil. With swift thinking by Gaelin and Peet, she was able to claw back her life and return to the land of the living - afraid and in pain, but alive.

Surprising herself, she didn't even flinch when she saw the lycanthrope standing in front of them. Something about him seemed kind, gentle, protective, and were it not for Ginger pulling her into the inn, she might have even tried to speak with him. But, surely such creatures are just bloodthirsty monsters, right?

Upon learning the story of the lycanthropes protecting their village from the party, she felt guilt over running away from him and treating him with fear and vowed to help with the celebration by cooking up a feast alongside her wife and Petee. With Petee's help, they were all able to have the dishes done just in time for the celebration to begin. Seeing everyone come together in unity she couldn't help but trill in excitement; lycanthropes and anthros working together, who'd have thought it?
