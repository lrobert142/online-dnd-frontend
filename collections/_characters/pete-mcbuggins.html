A quiet and stoic retired adventurer who is never seen without his wolf companion, Wolp. His keen eyes, firm but fair attitude, and solid sense of right and wrong have lead to him being the one many people look up to when in times of need.

After retiring from adventuring to settle down to a quiet life and return to his farming roots, he set up a small home in the forest to enjoy his retirement in peace. However, over time people began to join him and eventually he established a small village called Wolfhaven which he acts as the mayor for.

He has one son - Petee McBuggins - whom he cares for deeply.

Strangely enough nobody has ever seen him with the mother - or any woman for that matter - and Pete himself refuses to answer any questions about the topic.

Upon his son returning to the village, Pete was made aware of the lycanthropes guarding Wolfhaven and his son's many victories and incredible stories. He is happy to see his son not only doing so well, but also enjoying his life of adventuring and being with a such an amazing and capable group; allaying many of his worries. Though he wouldn't mind Petee coming back to visit again sometime.

During this visit, Pete and Petee also had a deep heart-to-heart discussion about his mother, though exact details seem to be a secret to everyone else. What could they have talked about?

The cultist attack on Wolfhaven took them both by surprise and though they were quick to act and put down the threat, Mango was sadly slain before anyone could step in and help. By Peet would not accept an end like that, and remembering a very old Scroll of Revivify he kept from his adventuring days "just in case" he and Gaelin were able to bring her back. All that left was the new difficulty: Darax, and him revealing the lycanthrope pack.

If you'd have told him that in a few short days the village would go from being terrified of lycanthropes to openly eating, drinking, and partying alongside them, he'd have had you checked for a mind-control spell. And yet, this was the reality, for the party was able to turn a tradgedy into a triumph and convince the residents of Wolfhaven to coexist with these powerful yet peaceful creatures. Sure, the housing arrangements will be difficult but how hard could it possibly be?
