Calendars: Tracking the Passing of Time

Report composed by Ferren Aetherwinter, Tsendoric Chronographer, 7689ATS

Time is important in the Material Plane and serves as a key resource many sentient creatures must spend wisely. So precious is it, that despite our differences, despite borders and political disagreements, all creatures of the Material Plane have agreed upon a single unified calendar with a consistent number of days and months.

A year is composed of 12 months, a month is composed of 5 weeks, a week is composed of 6 days. Thus a year is 360 days, and a month of 30 days. Thus does time remain consistent and easily memorable.

Days of the Week

A week is 6 days. Although some scholars indicate it was originally five until The God of Justice, Tyr, fought to gift us a sixth with which to bring his light of justice to the world.

  • Luxday (Lux): Lux (Light) is the light of dawn of a new week, a fresh start to new challenges
  • Arday (Ard): From the ancient Arcane spell Ardis, which brings knowledge and respect in equal parts
  • Ornday (Orn): Named after the Orntrix flowers which strangely only bloom on this day
  • Tyrday (Tyr): A day gifted from the God of Justice to allow us to make ammends for any wrongdoings
  • Velday (Vel): Veleray, the original creator of the modern calendar, has this day dedicated to them
  • Nyxday (Nyx): Nyx (Darkness) ends our week in twilight, a time of rest before a new beginning

Months of the Year

A year is split evenly into 12 months of equal length (30 days). While some months have changed names over time, there has always been a consistent 12 months in a year. Scholars are still trying to understand the origins of this decision.

Each year also has four seasons, split evenly with 3 months each.

  • Solumn ☀️
  • Dawn's Light: The dawn of a new year, and a fresh start for those who celebrate it
  • Sanctus: A holy month filled with reverence and worship of the sun
  • Emberwake: Supposedly named after the founding of Evocation magic
  • Autumnus 🍂
  • Flamebright: A month renowned for its expressions of Arcane magic of all forms
  • Crownfall: Celebrated as the month in which the terrible Lich King fell
  • Runeharvest: Runes seem to gather more power during this month
  • Glaciux ❄️
  • Misttide: A chilling mist begets the cold of the season
  • Wyrmrest: Believed to be the primary time during which dragonkin sleep
  • Lumenfall: The darkest month of the year, a cold and unnerving period
  • Naturia 🌱
  • Hallowtide: A month where otherwordly spirits can more easily cross into this plane
  • Radiant Wake: The spirits are dismissed in the wake of of this radiant month
  • Lliira's Joy: Lliira graces the end of the year and brings presents to those who deserve it

BTS & ATS

The history of the Material Plane is long and ofttimes quite bloody. The most well-known - and devastating - event in recoded history is a terrible catastrophy known as "The Sundering". While this report will not go into details, it must be noted that the event was so catastrophic that history has been split into two major time periods: BTS (Before The Sundering) and ATS (After The Sundering).

The oldest found piece of found history is believed to be a shard of an unknown artefact from approximately 45,000BTS, although validities of this claim is still in question. In time we hope to learn more of the relatively unknown past.

Regardless, this scholar believes that all should be aware of the past and learn of it so that we might never have such a horrifying experience occur again.

Lunar Cycles

The moon as we know it goes through 8 distinct phases, starting with a full moon on the 1st of the month, migrating to a darkened new moon by the 16th, and then making its way back to its full and resplendent glory.

The light ebbs and flows throughout the month, and is believed to be due to the eternal war between the holy goddess of the moon, Selûne, and the cowardly goddess of darkness, Shar. The two are said to be locked in a fight to control the great celestial body, which boosts the power of those who follow them.
