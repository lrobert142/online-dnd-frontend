By no means the most complex or secure cyphers, Kalyptic Cyphers are nonetheless incredibly useful tools for those who wish to hide their secrets from prying eyes. Using simple letter substitution and math, a normal message can be converted to near-unreadable nonsense in very little time, and converted back just as soon, provided you know the key.

For those that don't know the key, it can take a significant amount of time to properly decode - or guess - the key, and while not impossible to do so this time loss can be all that is needed for the original owner to notice the missing message and alert the authorities.

Indeed, it is very common for bards to use this type of cypher to ensure others do not steal their poems, songs, or stories. The time wasted by others decoding their ballads often leads to them getting caught, which in turn makes for yet more fascinating stories!
