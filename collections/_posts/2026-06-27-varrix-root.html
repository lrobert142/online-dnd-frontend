An unusual stick-like root that tastes both sweet and savoury at the same time, Varrix Roots are sometimes added to teas and simple soups in order to add that extra 'kick' to otherwise bland tasting food and drink. It is perfectly safe to consume, even the 'bark' itself is perfectly edible, however it tends to be quite firm and difficult to chew unless thoroughly boiled, which removes most of the flavour.

This inherent toughness, however, makes it ideal for dogs and other canines which absolutely adore the tasty and sturdy treat! Giving a dog - or wolf, as some have reported wild wolves enjoying it thoroughly as well - is a sure-fire way to make a quick friend and keep them busy for many hours. Very useful if you have a puppy that is teething, and a great way to avoid them chewing on valuables instead.

Thankfully these roots are also very easy to cultivate and thus are available across all of Qeenek at minimal cost. As the saying goes: "As long as there is soil to grow, Varrix Roots will prosper."
