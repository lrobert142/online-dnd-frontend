“The only constant is change!” - a popular saying about The Feywild

The Feywild is a vast plane of powerful creatures known as Fey for which their lands are known to constantly shift, change, and appear and disappear in the space of moments. For such a realm of uncertainty it may seem reasonable to map it out, to provide order in chaos and guide those unfortunate enough to find themselves in such a strange, vibrant, and unusual location.

Alas, you would not be the first to consider such things and unfortunately a map simply cannot exist for the Feywilds, so rapid is the changes instigated in this land.

At one point the entire Cartographer’s Guild set out to map the entire plane and force Material Plane order on the roiling chaos of The Feywild. And after 10 long years of constant work, they succeeded!

Whereupon the various Archfey changed the layout of their lands, swapped them with each other, stepped down while others stepped up, and made the maps completely incorrect and outdated in a single day. All the while laughing at the guild for their hubris.

For those unaware, Archfey refers to a Fey creature - any Fey creature actually - that has enough power to lay claim to a part of the plane. Once they have that control they can do whatever they want; grow their influence, shrink it, change the landscape, invert physics, etc. The only limit is imagination! And what other Archfey will let them get away with: trickery, subterfuge, and assassinations are common for those who cannot keep within their own bounds.

The only known limitation of an Archfey and their land is that they must represent something. Whether physical (fire, stone, swords, etc.) or abstract (order, invention, that-rock-you-find-in-your-shoe-at-2am-but-cannot-manage-to-shake-out-no-matter-how-hard-you-try) an Archfey can choose to represent it!

It is worth noting that because of how often the Feywilds change, those within are often bored quickly or expect constant change, and the desire for new things and expressions are common. Thus even Archfeys may step down suddenly for no reason other than “I got bored.”
